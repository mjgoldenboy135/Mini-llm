04 — Under the Hood
Architecture: This is an N-gram language model — the simplest form of a language model, predating deep learning. It's the conceptual ancestor of GPT.
1. Tokenization — Split text into individual characters (vocab)
2. N-gram counting — Count how often each sequence of N chars appears
3. Probability estimation — P(next_char | context) = count(context+char) / count(context)
4. Laplace smoothing — Add small count to unseen pairs to avoid zero probability
5. Temperature sampling — Divide log-probs by T, softmax, then sample
6. Perplexity — exp(avg negative log likelihood) — lower = better model fit
